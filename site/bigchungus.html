Big Chungus

Big Chungus is a funny fat rabbit from the hit show Looney Toons.
The main premise of the joke of making Bugs Bunny fat can be seen as obscure or even offensive
at first, however once you look at it from a lore perspective, it all makes
sense. You see, seconds before Bugs Bunny inflated himself into a balloon-like creature,
a man was nailing down a lid for a pit he put Bugs in. The caricature of the man was a
stereotypical fat American, and perhaps a satirical poke at the bourgeoisie, as there have been
many "memes" about communist Bugs Bunny. Once the fat American mocks an innocent rabbit,
Bugs Bunny instantly lifts the lid effortlessly, not bending or tearing a
single nail. He then proceeds to crawl out of the hole, turn around, and mock the
capitalist! Big Chungus shows a crude display of mockery for the upper class: his first-hand
insight on the toils they cause him, and the greed and sheer hatred for rabbits all reinforce this
belief and idea, this critique of endless cruelty and forced labor, endless consumerism; Big Chungus
sits back and simply mocks it all, he does not care enough to cause significant harm yet is amused
by the idea; he mocks the supreme authority (those with power, such as a hammer), displays disrespect
for their societal roles, all while barely trying. By lifting the wooden manhole without any effort,
he further reinforces the idea that force simply cannot always win, and wits combined with cleverness
always outsmart and overcome.

Thesis Statement

This scene is perhaps Warner Bros' magnum opus, as it incorporates deeper a political meaning in everyday pop culture.
